01Open & Wellbeing~5 min▼
Overall feeling this week (/10)
Why ask this
Sets the emotional tone for everything. A 4/10 changes how you read missed sessions — it's context, not failure. Never rush past this question.
Sample answers
"7/10 — work's been hectic but training felt like a release."
"4/10 — tired from travel, didn't sleep well Mon–Wed."
1–3 = Struggling · 4–6 = Moderate · 7–10 = Good
Sleep quality this week (/10)
Why ask this
One bad night is noise. Three in a row is a signal. If consistently below 5, pull intensity back — you can't force adaptation in a sleep-depleted athlete.
Energy levels across the week (/10)
Why ask this
Distinct from sleep. Someone can sleep 9hrs and feel flat — nutrition, stress, and accumulated fatigue show up here.
Any stress, illness, or life context I should know about?
Why ask this
Life load IS training load. A missed session during deadline week is a scheduling win, not a failure.
Muscle soreness or fatigue right now (/10)
Why ask this
Persistent 7+ soreness two days post-session = accumulated fatigue. Your primary cue to progress / maintain / reduce load.
02Last Week's Training~5 min▼
Sessions completed vs. planned
Coach tip
Arrive knowing this from the app. Use the call to understand WHY — not to discover WHAT.
/
Which session felt best — and why?
Win-surfacing
Probe past "it felt good." What specifically? These moments become content and build confidence.
🏆 Win capture
Which session felt hardest or worst — and why?
Pattern-spot, not blame
Same day of week? After a match? That's a programming insight — adjust scheduling, don't criticise.
RPE trend — did sessions feel appropriately hard? (/10)
Cross-check app data
Consistently too easy = progress load. Everything 9-10 = check recovery before adding more.

04Physical Status & Injury~2 min▼
Any pain, tightness, or niggles this week?
Ask every week
Athletes normalise discomfort. Catch niggles at 2/10 before they become 8/10 injuries.
Anything carried over from last week?
Track the trend
Worse = modify or refer. Same = manage load. Three weeks of the same = refer to physio.
Court load this week
Total load picture
Court load is part of ACWR. Heavy match week + heavy gym = spike risk. See TOTAL load.
05On-Court Performance~3 min▼
How did you move on court this week? (/10)
S&C transfer check
Consistently low despite good gym numbers = the exercises aren't transferring. Enough lateral work?
🏆 Win capture
Did anything from gym training show up in your game?
The bridge question
THE most important retention question. When athletes feel training working on court, they never drop off.
🏆 Win capture📸 Content potential
Any matches this week? Result + how body held up?
Result is secondary
Did they cramp? Fatigue in the final set? Match data tells you things gym data can't.

07Programme Fit & Feedback~2 min▼
Anything in the programme confusing or hard to follow?
Clarity = compliance
If the same thing confuses multiple athletes, it's an app or cue problem, not an athlete problem.
Did any exercise feel wrong or load incorrectly?
Movement quality flag
Don't dismiss discomfort as technique. Swap or regress — never push through "feels wrong."
More or less of anything? (ask every 3–4 weeks)
Preference check
Ask monthly, not weekly. Genuine preference data surfaces insights that improve adherence.
