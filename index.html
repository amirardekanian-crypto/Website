Common Questions

Before You Apply

The questions athletes ask me most — before we ever speak.

Do I need access to a gym?

Ideally yes, but a home-gym setup works too. During your intake call we map out exactly what equipment you have or can get, and I build the programme around that. You don't need a fully-kitted commercial gym to get real, measurable results.

I travel for tournaments — how does that work?

Travel is built into the plan from day one. Your programme includes travel-friendly sessions that can be done with minimal equipment, plus competition-week tapering so you arrive at tournaments feeling sharp — not smashed from a heavy lifting session two days earlier.

Can I combine this with my existing tennis or padel coach's programming?

Yes — and I strongly encourage it. Strength & conditioning is the layer underneath your on-court work, not a replacement for it. I'll happily coordinate load with your tennis coach if useful, so your gym and court sessions work together instead of fighting each other.

What if I get injured mid-programme?

We adapt. I've worked through hundreds of in-season niggles and injuries with athletes. Your programme gets adjusted immediately — often maintaining or even building strength in areas that aren't affected, so you come back fitter than you left.

How much time per week does the programme take?

Typically 2–4 sessions per week, 45–60 minutes each. The exact volume is calibrated to your on-court hours, your season phase, and how your body is responding — not a rigid template. Most athletes find it slots neatly around their existing training.

How is this different from a generic workout app?

An app gives you a workout. I give you a coach. Your programme is designed for your body and your competitive calendar, reviewed and adjusted by me every month, and delivered through an interactive platform where your data feeds directly into the next block of training. There's a human being on the other end of it — that's the whole point.
