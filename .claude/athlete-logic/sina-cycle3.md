# Sina Razeghi — Cycle 3 Design Logic

*Internal coaching record. Kept in `.claude/` so GitHub Pages does not publish it.
Athlete-specific reasoning — not a generalizable principle (those live in
`COACHING-PRINCIPLES.md`). Written so a future cycle can pick up the thread.*

> **The reusable framework — "conservative labrum management cycle":** protect the joint
> by removing the at-risk pattern entirely, build the *dynamic* stabilizers to compensate
> for the compromised *passive* one, train the rest of the body hard in the freed-up
> window, and give the athlete a self-governing pain rule so unsupervised training stays
> safe. Everything below is that framework applied to Sina.

---

## 0. The situation (Cycle 3 start)

- **MRI:** 30% anterior (front) labrum tear. Doctor prescribed surgery; said it can be
  **conservatively managed**. Decision: go conservative.
- **We are his only rehab.** No physio in the loop — so the cuff/scap work is OUR job,
  not a supplement to someone else's.
- **Zero logged data.** He has never logged a set. No RPE history, no real loads. The only
  number we have is coach-reported: **back-squats ~30–40 kg** (a true novice on the lift).
- Prior plan (locked roadmap Cycle 3 = **"Rotational Engine"**, med-ball power) is now
  **contraindicated** and was overridden for this cycle. Roadmap array left untouched.

## 1. The hard constraint → what comes OUT

Anterior labrum is most vulnerable in **abduction + external rotation under load**, and worst
when speed is added. So we removed:
- All **pressing** (overhead, incline, flat — dumbbell/barbell/machine alike)
- All **rotational power** — med-ball slams, wall slams, rotational throws
- Any **loaded anterior reach** / open-chain anterior loading

Nothing in the program puts the joint in the at-risk position under load.

## 2. The opportunity → what comes IN

Two moves to compensate for a compromised *passive* stabilizer (the labrum): build the
*dynamic* stabilizers, and spend the freed recovery capacity on the legs.

- **Dynamic shoulder stability** becomes a main event, not a finisher → a **dedicated Day 4**
  (cuff + scapula + serratus + rhythmic stabilization under carry load).
- **Lower body is the cycle driver** — two lower sessions (squat-bias + hinge-bias) carry
  the real training stimulus this block.

## 3. Structure — why 4 days

3 days would have forced the cuff work to be a tail-end afterthought on an upper day.
A torn labrum with us as the only rehab does not get an afterthought. So:

| Day | Focus | Load identity |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Lower — **squat bias** (quad) | moderate-high |
| 2 | Upper — **pull only, zero press** + carries | moderate |
| 3 | Lower — **hinge bias** (post. chain) | moderate-high |
| 4 | **Shoulder rehab & stability** (the main event) | low systemic cost |

Undulation check: no two heavy lower days back-to-back; Day 4 is deliberately low-cost so
the cuff work is fresh, not fatigued.

## 4. Lower-body prescription — anchored to reality, not the textbook

First instinct was a "strength cycle" 4×5 @ RPE 7–8. **Rejected.** At 30–40 kg he is a
novice on the pattern — 4×5 near-max grinds a skill he hasn't earned and builds little.

Locked instead:
- **Back Squat** and **Barbell RDL** as the two primaries (both seen in Cycle 2).
- **3×8 @ RPE 6–7, with tempo.** Build skill + load tolerance; **add weight weekly** from a
  coachable start. Strength shows up as kilos on a clean pattern, not low-rep grinding.
- This call became the generalizable principle: *"Anchor selection, rep range, AND dose to
  the athlete's REAL numbers"* (COACHING-PRINCIPLES.md, Progression, 2026-06-27).

## 5. Day 4 built as real rehab (not cuff theatre)

- **Isolated external rotation** ×3 vectors — Side-Lying DB ER, Band ER, Cable ER
  (infraspinatus / teres minor under different load lines).
- **Serratus Wall Slide** — scapular control; a torn labrum with a weak serratus gets worse.
- **Incline Y-T-W Raise** — lower/mid trap + serratus, full scapular health.
- **Bear Crawl + Suitcase Carry** — rhythmic stabilization: hold shoulder position while the
  body moves = the real-world demand.
- ER work programmed light (RPE ~6), tempo'd, controlled. No pressing, no overhead, no
  loaded anterior reach anywhere in the day.

## 6. The pain traffic-light (non-negotiable, in his notes)

He trains unsupervised, so he needs a rule he can apply mid-session without calling:
- 🟢 **0–3/10 dull ache** → continue
- 🔴 **sharp / pinching / anterior** pain, OR pain lasting **>24 h** → stop that movement,
  message Amir.

This rule is what keeps conservative management from quietly becoming a surgical case.

## 7. Engagement layer (written last, after the program existed)

Framed the labrum **honestly — a project we manage smartly, not a setback.** Notes span:
training/technique, the pain rule, **logging Week-1 baselines** (we have none),
**sleep** (cuff/tendon remodelling is sleep-dependent), recovery, and **padel load** on
off days. In-app text English; tone direct, coach-to-athlete.

## 8. Open items / how to drive next week

- **No logged data yet — Week 1 IS the baseline.** Whatever he squats and RDLs that first
  session is the anchor for everything after. Read those logs before any Week-2 change.
- Progression lever: add load weekly to Back Squat + RDL while the pattern stays clean at
  RPE 6–7; only chase reps/RPE once load tolerance is established.
- Shoulder: if pain stays 🟢 and ER strength climbs, the *next* cycle can begin a **staged
  return** (isometric → eccentric → full ROM → loaded → reactive) — but reactive/rotational
  power stays parked until the shoulder has clearly earned it.
- Roadmap "Rotational Engine" theme is deferred, not deleted — revisit when the labrum is
  asymptomatic under load.
