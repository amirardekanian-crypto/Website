*Move Better. Hit Harder. Last Longer.*

This is the full picture of what you're signing up for: not a PDF, not a generic
plan you download and figure out alone — **a coaching relationship**, built around
you, delivered through your own private app, and adjusted every single week based on

Everything we do follows one rule: **nothing without a reason.** Every exercise,

we'll always tell you what that purpose is.

## The short version

1. **We get to know you** — your goals, your life, your injuries, your schedule, your equipment, how you recover.
2. **We analyse** — we line your goals up, resolve any that pull against each other, and (if you're an athlete) run a proper needs analysis for your sport.
3. **We build your six months** — a science-based programme shaped around *your* constraints, mapped cycle by cycle, with a clear reason and a clear outcome for each one.

Below is the long version — the part that explains *why* this works.

## Step 1 — We get to know you (Discovery)

Before a single exercise is chosen, we gather the information that actually changes
how you should train. When you apply, you fill in a structured intake that covers:

- **Your goals** — get stronger, faster, more powerful, injury-proof, leaner, back from an injury, or performing on court — and the one specific thing you most want to achieve.
- **Your sport & level** — for tennis and padel players, where you play and how often, because the court is part of your training load.
- **Your training history** — how long you've trained, what you do now, and (optionally) your current best lifts, so we know your real starting point.
- **Your body** — current pain or injuries, past injuries and surgeries that still affect you, and any movements you've been told to avoid.
- **Your recovery capacity** — sleep quality and hours, stress, nutrition, and how demanding your week is outside the gym. This matters more than most people think (see Step 3).
- **Your constraints** — how many days a week you can train, how long each session can be, and exactly what equipment you have access to.

We ask all of this because **a great programme you can't actually do is a bad
programme.** Your real life is a design input, not an afterthought.

---

## Step 2 — We analyse before we prescribe (Analysis)

Information on its own isn't a plan. This is where the coaching starts.

**We line up your goals.** Some goals stack neatly together; some compete for the
same recovery budget. If you want to get noticeably stronger, more powerful, *and*
bulletproof your joints on two 30-minute sessions a week, we'll be honest about what
that realistically buys you — and either help you find a little more time, or
prioritise the goal that matters most right now and sequence the rest. We'd rather
tell you the truth up front than promise everything and deliver none of it.

**We respect the order the body demands.** Some things gate others. If your knee
hurts, we don't chase top-end speed through it — we calm the knee down first, then
build the speed on a foundation that won't break. Health and capacity come before
performance, every time.

**For athletes, we run a needs analysis.** If you're a tennis or padel player, we
look at what the sport actually demands of your body — the sprints and changes of
direction, the rotational power behind every shot, the shoulder and hip health that
keeps you on court, the repeat-effort endurance to still be sharp in the third set —
and we work backwards from *that* to decide what your training has to deliver. Your
programme is built to transfer to the court, not just to look good in the gym.

---

## Step 3 — We build your six months (Programming)

Now we design — and we design around you, on the best available science.

**Your programme is a journey, not a single workout.** It's a chain of linked
**cycles** (roughly five weeks each), and each cycle is a mission with its own focus,
its own reason for existing, and a clear outcome you should walk away with. One cycle
might lay your foundation; the next builds raw strength; the next turns that strength
into power; the next armours you against injury. **We plan all six months up front**,
so you can see what you'll be doing each month, what you'll have achieved by the end
of it, and why that step matters before the next one.

**We pick exercises that give *you* the best return.** For most goals we choose the
movements with the highest payoff for the lowest cost — the ones that build the most
without burying you in fatigue or risk. For athletes, we'll sometimes choose a
harder movement on purpose because it transfers better to your sport — and when we
do, we'll tell you why. Either way, the choice is deliberate, matched to your
equipment and your body.

**Recovery sets the ceiling.** Here's the part most plans ignore: your ability to
recover decides how much training you can actually turn into progress. That's why we
asked about your sleep, stress, nutrition, and the demands of your week. If your life
is heavy, we don't just pile on more volume and hope — we shape the week intelligently,
so the hard days are genuinely hard and the easy days let you absorb the work. The
programme fits your recovery, not someone else's ideal.

**Every prescription is specific.** Each exercise comes with its sets and reps, a
target effort level (RPE), tempo, and rest — so you always know exactly what the
session is asking of you.

## Step 4 — You train, through your own app

When you start, you get a private link that installs **AA Performance** on your phone
like any other app — *a coach in your pocket.* This is where your programme lives, and
it's built so you're never guessing.

**Before each session — a quick wellbeing check.** You rate how you slept, your
energy, your soreness, your stress, and how you feel overall. It takes seconds, and it
does two things: it lets the session adapt to how you actually walked in that day, and
it shows your coach exactly how you're holding up.

**During the session — everything you need on the card.** Tap any exercise and you
get a **demo video**, the full prescription (sets, reps, target, tempo, rest), and
**coaching cues** — what to do, what to feel, and the one mistake to avoid. No
guessing what an exercise is or how it should look.

**As you go — you log your work.** Each set, you record the weight, your effort (RPE),
and tick it off. A built-in session timer and rest timer keep you moving. When you
finish, you rate the session, leave a note if you want, and send it all to your coach.

That log isn't busywork — it's the raw material your coach uses to make your next week
better.

---

## Step 5 — We watch, we talk, we adjust (the coaching loop)

This is the half that makes it *coaching* and not just an app.

**Your coach watches your data.** Everything you log flows to a private coach
dashboard — your progress, your adherence, your readiness trend, and your training
load. It flags early warning signs (a drop in readiness, a spike in load, a quiet
week) so problems get caught *before* they become injuries or burnout. The promise
behind the product is simple: **before you get hurt, your coach messages you.**

**You get a real weekly check-in.** Every week, we go through how you're doing —
wellbeing, sleep, energy, soreness, how you're responding, your wins, your niggles,
and what you want next — over a call or WhatsApp. It's a relationship, not a file drop.

**Your plan keeps changing as you do.** Based on everything coming in — your logs,
your readiness, your check-ins — we adjust. If a movement doesn't suit you, hurts, or
you simply can't do it, we swap it and note *why*, so it doesn't come back unless it's
genuinely right for you. At the end of each cycle, all of that week-by-week
information is read together to decide what the next cycle should do. You're never
running a stale plan.

**Two-way messaging is always open.** Your coach is a message away inside the app,
alongside personal notes written for you and an in-app guide if you need it.

Your programme is the priority, but the app's **Library** also gives you a shelf of
ready-to-run **Sessions** — standalone workouts, drills, and resets you can do on your

exercise cards. They're a bonus, not an obligation — there whenever you want them.
